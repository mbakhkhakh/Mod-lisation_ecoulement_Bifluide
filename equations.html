In a two-fluid model, each fluid is distinct and has its own unique properties, specifically density (ρ) and viscosity (μ). These fluids adhere to the principles of the Navier-Stokes equations.This model can be expressed through the following formulation:

$$ \frac{\partial u_{1}}{\partial t}+ (u_{1}.\nabla) u_{1}= -\frac{1}{\rho_{1}}\nabla p_{1}+ \frac{1}{\rho_{1}}\nabla . \mu_{1}(\nabla u_{1}+\nabla u_{1}^{T} )+ F_{s1} $$

$$ \frac{\partial u_{2}}{\partial t}+ (u_{2}.\nabla) u_{2}= -\frac{1}{\rho_{2}}\nabla p_{2}+ \frac{1}{\rho_{2}}\nabla . \mu_{2}(\nabla u_{2}+\nabla u_{2}^{T} )+ F_{s2} $$

A single momentum equation can be formulated using the new variables ρ and μ to represent and solve a unified problem:

$$ \frac{\partial u}{\partial t}+(\mathbf{u} \cdot \nabla) u = -\frac{1}{\rho}\nabla p + \frac{1}{\rho}\nabla \cdot \mu( \nabla \mathbf{u} + \nabla \mathbf{u}^T) + F_s $$

In this model, (ρ, μ) are functions that vary depending on the fluid. Specifically, they equal (ρ₁, μ₁) in the first fluid and (ρ₂, μ₂) in the second. To accurately represent the interface Γ between these two fluids, the boundary is defined as the zero level of a function φ, known as the LevelSet, where:

$$ \Gamma = \{x \,|\, \phi(x, t) = 0\} $$

The LevelSet is dynamically updated at each step of the computation by solving the subsequent Transport equation:

$$ \dfrac{\partial \phi}{\partial t} + \mathbf{u} \cdot \nabla \phi = 0 $$

Initially, the LevelSet is chosen to be the signed distance function relative to the interface:

$$ \phi(x, t = 0) = \pm d $$

Subsequently, the values of ρ and μ are reconstructed from the LevelSet after being updated using the following method:

$$ \rho = \rho_1 + (\rho_2 - \rho_1)F(\phi) $$

$$ \mu = \mu_1 + (\mu_2 - \mu_1)F(\phi) $$
